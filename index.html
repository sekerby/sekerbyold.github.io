Welcome! My name is Steve Kerby, I'm a 4th-year PhD student at the Penn State University Dept. of Astronomy and Astrophysics, currently advised and mentored by Dr. Abe Falcone. My research focuses on using multiwavelength observations to characterize enigmatic high-energy objects in the distant universe.

Using archival data and new observations from the Niel Gehrels Swift Observatory, my research centers on building broadband spectra of unclassified gamma-ray objects like the Fermi unassociated sources. After characterizing the multiwavelength emission of each object, I use machine learning tools like random forests and neural networks to decipher each source's classification and intrinsic properties.

Outside of research, I have contributed to scientific outreach programs for the past three years, mentored several undergraduates, and designed and taught ASTRO 1: The Astronomical Universe in summer of 2022. I enjoy hiking, playing Dungeons and Dragons, martial arts, and playing the Bass.
